Since 2009, at least 85 Florida autopsies have listed excited delirium as a cause of death.
Each square represents one of them.

In most cases, law enforcement used force on the victim prior to their death.

The majority of victims were also under the influence of illegal stimulant drugs — like cocaine or methamphetamine — when they died.

In almost every case in which drugs weren't involved, police had used force on the victim.

In almost half of police-involved cases, law enforcement had used a taser on the victim prior to their death.

But most deaths — even the ones involving use of force — were classified as accidents.

Of the 53 excited delirium cases involving law enforcement, 41 listed the manner of death as "accident".

"Accident" was also listed as the manner of death for four out of five cases where tasers were involved.
